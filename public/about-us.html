The SI Sandy Relief Fund is a registered 501(C)(3) charity. The founders of SI Sandy Relief Fund are friends most of whom either originated from Staten Island or currently live there. We all have families and can sympathize with the people affected by SuperStorm Sandy and decided to donate our hearts, time and contacts to help good people get back on their feet. We do not make or take a single penny from any of the donations made. In fact the only costs to run the organization were funded by the founders themselves. Below you can find bios and contact information for all of our founders and members. 

Bradley Vaiana (Board MemBER)

Brad Vaiana grew up in the New Springville section of Staten Island. Brad graduated Port Richmond High School in 1993.  Following high school Brad attended Binghamton University where he graduated in 1997 cum laude with a double major in English and Political Science.  Brad obtained his law degree from Emory University, graduating in 2000 with highest honors.  

Brad is currently a partner in the private equity group at Winston & Strawn LLP, a highly regarded international law firm.  He is currently serving as the Co-Chair of the Global Private Equity Group and is a member of the firm’s audit committee.  Brad’s clients are some of the largest private equity funds, corporations and alternative asset managers in the world.  Brad is currently serving as outside pro bono counsel to the Newark Mentorship Movement, a 501c3 organization based out of Newark, New Jersey that was formed to implement educational reforms put forth by Newark Mayor Corey Booker. 

Brad currently resides in Wyckoff New Jersey with his wife Nadine and three children, Geoffrey (7), Jolie (5) and Jordan (1).  Brad continues to have significant  ties to Staten Island with family and friends on the Island.  He is an active member of the Wyckoff community coaching his son’s soccer team and football team.  When not working Brad’s favorite thing to do is spend time outside, be it hiking, mountain climbing, snowboarding, mountain biking or rock climbing.

lee maschler (board member)

Lee Maschler grew up in Heartland Village on Staten Island. Lee graduated from Port Richmond High School in 1993.  Following high school Lee attended Cornell University where he graduated in 1997 and is currently a visiting lecturer for the Cornell Financial Engineering in Manhattan master’s program.
